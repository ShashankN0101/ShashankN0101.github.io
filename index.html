Project Scope Management — Boeing 787 Global Supply Chain Modernization
Northeastern University · Project Scope Management · Academic Case Study
Completed Scope Management

Examined Boeing's 787 Dreamliner program as a landmark case in global supply chain scope management failure and recovery. The program — spanning 40+ Tier 1 suppliers across 10 countries — suffered multi-year delays and cost overruns exceeding $32B, driven by scope creep, distributed accountability, and insufficient integration management at the prime contractor level.

Applied PMBOK Scope Management processes to diagnose where Boeing's WBS broke down: over-decomposition of work packages to suppliers without adequate interface control, absence of a centralized scope baseline, and a change control process that lacked executive enforcement. Developed a corrective scope management framework including an integrated WBS with supplier-level traceability, a formal scope baseline document, and a change control board structure with defined authority thresholds.
